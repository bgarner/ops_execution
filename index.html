Operations Exectuion Document - FY16

This document covers the timing / schedules / format that must be followed by all business partners to ensure that the stores receive all required information to run their day to day business. Please ensure that this is used as a daily tool.

The information in this document replaces any current agreements / practices.

Pricing

PRICE CHANGE COMMUNICATION

  • Stores have visibility to price changes via their Item Price Change report available on the Corporate Web Portal. (Emails regarding price changes are not required unless deemed so by Cam Munro).

PRICE EVENT ENTRY DEADLINES

  • Ad Pricing (Temps and Perms) - Wed by 5PM (2 weeks ahead of ad start date).
  • Non-ad related Perm pricing - Wed by 5PM (5 days ahead of perm/clearance start date).
  • Any off schedule price events must be approved by Cam Munro.

CROSSOVER PROMOTIONS

  • Crossover promotions (an event to create uniform pricing between banners) must be communicated to Tara Graham two weeks prior to the event being active in order to communicate it to stores.
  • Note: It is the responsibility of purchasing to identify all crossover promotions (both advertised and unadvertised).

UNADVERTISED PROMOTIONS

  • Requests for unadvertised promotions must be approved by the Purchasing AVP and Cam Munro and must be submitted a minimum of 2 weeks in advance of being active.

ON-GOING PROMOTIONS

  • In the case of promotion extending beyond the original end date, Tara Graham must be informed as early as possible (preferably 1 week prior to ending date).
  • In the case of promotions with no predetermined end date (e.g. long term BOGO), Tara Graham must be informed a minimum of 1 week prior to the promotion end date in order to communicate to the stores.

.88 MARKDOWNS

  • Purchasing must notify Cam Munro a minimum of 3 weeks prior to proposed .88 markdown date to allow proper store notification.
  • There can be no .88 markdowns without Operations approval.
  • Purchasing AVPs to consolidate .88 markdowns of all their categories whenever possible.
  • .88 markdowns will be done a maximum of once per quarter unless otherwise approved.
  • .88 markdowns are to be active on Mondays only.
  • Pricing must be entered a minimum of 10 days prior to being active in stores.
  • All .88 markdowns for s seasonal product must be taken prior to the product being merchandised on the sales floor as part of the changeover process.

PRICE CHANGE SCHEDULE

In order to balance store workload it is very important that we strictly adhere to the markdown schedule.

  • Active Monday Perms (excluding FW perms) / Clearance.
    • Softgoods perms are to be active on EVEN fiscal weeks unless related to an ad.
    • Hardgoods perms are to be active on ODD fiscal weeks unless related to an ad.
    • Exceptions to the Monday active date:
      • If Monday is a holiday, perms are active on Tuesday.
      • Current temps going to perm which would go in on Tuesday to avoid the 1 day conflict (Tara Graham must be informed).
  • Active Tuesday - Temps & FW perms (FW perms active in odd fiscal weeks only unless related to an ad).
  • In the rare case where a temp price needs to be active on Wednesday (rather than Tuesday) in order to meet a time test, Tara Graham will need to be notified a minimum of 1 week prior in order to alert stores.
  • Active Wednesday - Missed Temps/no missed perms unless ad related (Tara Graham must be informed of all Wednesday markdowns).
  • Thursday to Sunday - none (unless approved by Purchasing AVP and Cam Munro).

GENERIC SIGNAGE / PACKAGE PRICING

  • All generic / package price signage (ski packages, camping packages, etc.) are created by head office. Stores can only create signage for single items.
  • Package price changes must be communicated to Tara Graham a minimum of 1 week before taking effect in order for new price signage to be created and made available to stores.

SEASONAL RE-TICKETS

  • Seasonal product re-tickets are scheduled 2 times per year – Purchasing AVPs will be notified of the dates in advance.
  • All re-ticket requests must be approved by the respective Purchasing AVP and consolidated into a single spreadsheet using the approved format and sent to Tara Graham and Cam Munro.
  • Purchasing is responsible for contacting the DC and e-comm to ensure all remaining units are re-ticketed.

TICKET CORRECTIONS

  • Details required: Style number, description, incorrect price, correct price, date effective (Mondays), reason for correction, list of stores with on-hand units (e.g. all SC stores or ATM stores, or provide list of select stores).
  • Use template available at J:\Operations Communication
  • Unless it is deemed an emergency (Operations / Purchasing decision), re-tickets will be communicated through the Friday Planning bulletins.
  • Timing of the re-ticket will be determined by Ops after a review of current store workload.

LIST PRICE CHANGES

  • List Price Adjustments need to be discussed with Operations prior to being entered.
  • Timing of list price changes will be determined by Ops after a review of current store workload.

Advertising

CORRECTION NOTICES

  • In the event a correction notice is required for an ad, the buyer/analyst is to email Vivian Akrofi the details (copy Angela Moccia). Vivian will create the correction notice and email to Tara Graham for distribution via the Operations Portal.

AD EXTENSIONS

  • In the event an ad is to be extended please notify Cam Munro by Monday 10AM MST in order to allow adequate time to inform the stores.

SUBSTITUTIONS

  • A sub is required for any store that does not meet the minimum required on-hand units for an advertised item when checked on the Monday of the week the ad breaks. The savings on the substitution must be of similar value as the original and the selling price cannot exceed that of the advertised item.
  • Purchasing must use sub approved template J:\Operations Communication. Subs lists not provided in the correct template will be sent back to be properly formatted.
  • Advertising Analysts are required to send sub lists to Tara Graham by 5pm on Monday of the week the ad breaks. If Monday is a holiday the information is due by 3pm on the previous Friday.
  • Subs will be communicated via the Operations Portal.

PRODUCT FEATURES AND BENEFITS

  • A minimum of 1 Feature and/or Benefit must be filled in under the Description field in Product Maintenance for all styles. (Exceptions include items such as certain basic accessories. Consult with Buyer and/or Purchasing AVP for further direction on this matter).
  • The description field contains 3 Feature lines that can accommodate up to 26 characters each.
  • All Features should be in UPPER CASE.

Product Movement

RECALLS

  • Large recalls / RTVs need to be discussed with Cam Munro a minimum of 2 weeks in advance of proposed timeline to allow for review of store workload.
  • Purchasing is required to inform the Warranty dept. of all recall details.
  • Recalls are communicated to stores via the Operations Portal on Monday's at 5am MST.
  • Blackout period dates (e.g. 3 weeks in Dec) will be communicated by Cam Munro.
  • Purchasing is responsible for contacting the DC to ensure remaining units are not sent to the stores.

REGROUPS

  • All regroups must be approved by Troy Lemens and Cam Munro and are sent out on Mondays at 5am MST via the Operations Portal.
    • Exceptions to the Monday timing are team sale regroups.
    • All regroups must be sent in the approved format only.
    • The timing of large regroups needs to be coordinated with Cam Munro a minimum of 3 weeks prior to proposed execution date.

Store Communication

WEEKLY COMMUNICATION (FRIDAY PLANNING BULLETINS)

  • All general communication is to go through Tara Graham.
    • Communication is given to stores 2 weeks in advance of any action being required by stores in order to give the stores enough time to plan workload.
    • Communication must be received by Tara Grahamby 10am on Wednesdays.
    • Communications are sent out via the Operations Portal.
  • Acceptable Purchasing to Store communication:
    • 1 on 1 communication with stores (e.g. special orders, product info, etc).
    • Unless initiated by the store, all purchasing communication is to take place prior to noon (MST).

PLAYBOOK (SPORT CHEK), TRAIL GUIDE (ATMOSPHERE)

  • Deadlines for submissions are Thursday 5PM 2 weeks prior to the ad starting (e.g. Week 8 Playbook info due on Thursday of Week 6).
  • Templates are available at: J:\Operations Communication\Templates for Submissions

PRODUCT LAUNCHES

  • Any styles that have a specific launch date must be communicated to Tara Grahama minimum of 2 weeks prior to the launch date.
  • It is critical that Operations receive launch detail prior to any product reaching stores/
  • Information required is:
    • PMM #, Vendor style #, description, image, launch date, retail price, list of receiving stores
    • Templates are available at J:\Operations Communication\Templates for Submissions

STORE CONTESTS

  • All contests are subject to Operations approval based on:
    • Focus of the contest.
    • Number of contests already planned for time period.
    • Number of stores involved in contest.
      • Contests that do not include all stores will only be considered when it is the launch of a new (important) product.
    • Effectiveness of proposed contest in driving sales.
  • In-store contest proposals must be sent to Cam Munro 4 weeks prior to proposed start date.
  • Contests cannot require an in store paper ballot or tracking system.
  • Contests can last a maximum of 4 weeks.
  • Operations will work with the vendor to secure and create contest assets.
  • Where possible the contest prizing must be sent to head office in advance of the contest ending (does not include contests where winners sizing requirements is necessary).
  • Excessive delays in receiving contest prizing will preclude the vendor from participating in future contests.

REP VISITS

  • All requests for vendor rep visits must be prearranged with Matt Dellandrea – Manager, Product Training prior to committing to committing to a vendor.
    • Matt will determine if a store visit from the vendor rep is warranted, based on product sales volume, seasonal timing, the store's familiarity with the product, existing FGL training etc.

PRODUCT KNOWLEDGE MATERIALS

  • Prior to agreeing to distribute any PK material to store please contact Matt Dellandrea, Manager Product Training.

Marketing

IN-STORE PROMOTIONS

Scratch and save cards – Marketing is required to:
  • Determining required quantities by store.
  • Develop creative for cards as well as any supporting POP.
  • Print and ship cards (including tracking of proof of delivery).
Promo gift cards – Marketing is required to:
  • Develop work back schedule to insure critical timelines are met.
  • Determining required quantities by store.
  • Develop creative for cards as well as any supporting POP.
  • Work with Operations on printing of cards.
  • Distribute cards to stores.
Rebate promotion – Marketing is required to:
  • Develop creative for any supporting POP that is not part of a store core kit (as seen in the POP clean up document).
  • Provide Tara Graham and Cam Munro with detailed store communication on program minimum of 2 weeks prior to the promotion starting.
  • Operations will coordinate printing and distribution of supporting POP.
  • Note: A minimum of 2 weeks is required to print and ship POP.
General Promotions (example: BTS easel or window / wall) – Marketing is required to:
  • Develop creative for any supporting POP that is not part of a store core kit (as seen in the POP clean up document).
  • Provide Tara Graham and Cam Munro with detailed store communication on the program minimum of 2 weeks prior to the promotion starting.
  • Operations will coordinate printing and distribution of supporting POP.
  • Note: A minimum of 2 weeks is required to print and ship POP.

MARKETING INITIATED CONTESTS

This includes external contests that are not executed in store.

  • Marketing must discuss any proposed contests with Purchasing in advance of presenting to Cam Munro to ensure Purchasing / Marketing alignment.
  • Develop creative for any supporting POP.
  • Provide Tara Graham and Cam Munro with a detailed store communication on the contest a minimum of 2 weeks prior to the start of the contest.
  • Operations will coordinate printing and distribution of supporting POP.
  • Note: A minimum of 2 weeks is required to print and ship POP.

MARKETING EVENTS (not in-store)

We want to ensure that our store teams are informed of all marketing events that may result in questions from customers. Please send Tara Graham and Cam Munro the details of all events a minimum of 2 weeks in advance of the event taking place.

IN STORE EVENTS

All in store player / celebrity appearances / events must be discussed with Cam Munro in advance of any detailed planning taking place.

  • Operations will work with the necessary business partners to plan the event.
